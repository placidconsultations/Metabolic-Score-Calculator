What's your real Metabolic Syndrome risk?

This calculator uses the siMS score — a single report-card number that adds up how your waist size, blood pressure, blood sugar, triglycerides and HDL cholesterol compare to healthy ranges. It's based on published research (Soldatović et al., PLOS ONE, 2016), plus the internationally recognised criteria for diagnosing Metabolic Syndrome. It needs a few real measurements — not guesses.

Have these ready

  • • Height & waist circumference (cm)
  • • Blood pressure reading
  • • A recent fasting blood test: glucose, triglycerides & HDL cholesterol

This tool is for education and screening only, and is not a replacement for your doctor or healthcare provider. It does not diagnose you — only a qualified professional can do that using your full clinical picture. If you don't have recent lab values, that's useful information too: it means it's time to get tested.

siMS score, in plain terms

Think of it as a single report-card number for five things that drive metabolic health: your waist size, blood pressure, blood sugar, triglycerides, and "good" (HDL) cholesterol. Each one is compared to its own healthy cut-off and added together into one score. A score around 3.0 means you're sitting right at the borderline on all five — not necessarily unhealthy, but right at the edge. Below that generally reflects a healthier profile; the further above 3.0, the more these factors are stacking up together. The siMS risk score takes that same number and adjusts it for your age and family history, since risk isn't just about today's numbers.

Not a replacement for your doctor

This calculator is an educational screening aid, not a diagnosis. It can't account for medications, other conditions, or the full picture a doctor sees. Please review these numbers with your doctor or a qualified healthcare provider before changing any treatment, medication, or diet — especially if any marker above is flagged.
